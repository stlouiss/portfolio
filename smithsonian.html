National Numismatic Collection

Smithsonian Institution,

National Museum of American History

Summer 2015



As an intern at the National Numismatic Collection, I worked in one of the world's largest collections of monetary objects.

One major highlight of the experience was contributing to cataloging projects, including for the Josiah K. Lilly, Jr. Collection, consisting of various gold coins and bars minted in the United States and around the world.

Additionally, I took advantage of living in Washington, DC, for the summer to pursue face-to-face networking opportunities with experts in archives and records management, federal grantmaking, information science, digital preservation, and public history programming at the Institute of Museum and Library Services, the National Archives and Records Administration, and the Library of Congress.

Below, please enjoy linked images of just a few objects in the vast holdings of the National Numismatic Collection. All links point to records in Wikimedia Commons describing the objects in greater detail. These images are in the public domain.
