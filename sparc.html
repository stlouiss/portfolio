Scholarly Publishing and Academic Resources Coalition

(SPARC)

Internship, May 2014 to July 2016

OpenCon Application Review Team, 2015-2018



As an intern for SPARC, I conducted community engagement work in many different forms.

For targeted outreach to student organizations, I compiled detailed membership information. I also advised academic librarians seeking to promote graduate and undergraduate student advocacy for Open Access and Open Educational Resources at their own institutions.

Serving on the application review teams for OpenCon 2015-2018, I was one of the top three reviewers overall in my final year. At that time, I evaluated more than 3000 applications from researchers and students in dozens of countries. In 2017, I was recognized with others by name at the Max Planck Society in Berlin as one of the top overall reviewers for the OpenCon conference.

On Capitol Hill in 2015, I engaged in legislative advocacy for the proposed Fair Access to Science and Technology Research Act (FASTR).

In 2014, I participated in a Congressional Advocacy Day for open scholarly communication. With my colleagues, I received guidance from the Deputy Assistant for Legislative Affairs to the President of the United States.
